Obsidian Vault

Personal Knowledge Base

My Obsidian vault is the closest thing I have to a second brain. Notes on everything bg-loop games, music, tech, studying, freelance work. A finance log. A project tracker. A library of every book and film I've gone through. It's fully private, but it's probably the most thought-through thing I've built.

Started in 2022, after moving away from Notion. I wanted something local - files I actually own, that aren't sitting on someone else's server.

FAQ

What's the structure?

Everything lives under _OBSIDIAN/databases/ - split into dedicated folders: notes/, projects/, tasks/, finances/, journals/, academics/, and library/. Each database has its own schema and Dataview queries. Yearly subfolders keep things from piling up.

Why Obsidian?

Local-first, plain markdown files, no subscription. I came from Notion and didn't like the idea of my journals sitting on someone else's servers. Everything here is mine.

Can I see it?

No. It's fully private and I'd like to keep it that way.

Why put it on your portfolio then?

Because I built it, and I've put real time into it. A portfolio doesn't have to be things other people can use.
